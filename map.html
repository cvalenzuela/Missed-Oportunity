Human Rights and U.S. Government Purchases in the Persian Gulf

Chapter One: The Context

The economic growth in oil-producing Gulf States like Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, the UAE, and Oman largely tracked in tandem with the price of oil, so that massive economic growth occurred between 1973 and 1980, followed by smaller contractions between 1980 and 1986, and steady but relatively stagnant growth between 1986 and 1998.

Following another dramatic price collapse in December 1998 to levels not seen since before 1973, the price of oil began an unprecedented and dramatic rise, peaking only in 2014.
